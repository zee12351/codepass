### QOTD #1 — Modifier 25
**🧠 CPC Question of the Day**
A patient comes in for a scheduled wart removal. During the visit they also report new chest tightness, and the physician performs a separate, significant E/M evaluation for it. How do you report the E/M alongside the procedure?

A) E/M only
B) Procedure only
C) E/M with modifier 25 + the procedure
D) E/M with modifier 59

Drop your answer 👇

**Answer: C.** Modifier 25 flags a *significant, separately identifiable* E/M performed on the same day as a procedure. The chest-tightness work-up is above and beyond the usual pre-procedure assessment, so append **modifier 25** to the E/M. Modifier 59 is for distinct *procedural* services, not E/M.

### QOTD #2 — ICD-10 specificity & laterality
**🧠 CPC Question of the Day**
Documentation says "primary osteoarthritis of the right knee." Which ICD-10-CM code is correct?

A) M17.0 — bilateral
B) M17.11 — unilateral, right
C) M17.9 — unspecified
D) M17.12 — unilateral, left

**Answer: B — M17.11.** Always code to the highest specificity the documentation supports, including laterality. "Right knee, primary OA" = M17.11. Reaching for M17.9 (unspecified) when laterality is clearly documented is a classic, avoidable error that triggers denials.

Train your eye for specificity → zarivo.in

### QOTD #3 — 2021+ E/M guidelines
**🧠 CPC Question of the Day**
Under the 2021 office/outpatient E/M guidelines (99202–99215), the visit level is chosen based on total time on the encounter date OR…?

A) Number of body systems examined
B) Medical Decision Making (MDM)
C) Severity of the chief complaint
D) Number of diagnoses alone

**Answer: B — MDM.** Since 2021, office/outpatient E/M is selected by **either total time** on the date of the encounter **or MDM**. History and exam no longer drive the code level — they just need to be medically appropriate. This change trips up a LOT of new coders.

Master the new E/M rules → zarivo.in

### QOTD #4 — HCPCS vs CPT
**🧠 CPC Question of the Day**
A provider administers an injection of dexamethasone. Which code set reports the *drug supply* itself?

A) CPT Category I
B) HCPCS Level II (J-code)
C) ICD-10-PCS
D) CPT Category III

**Answer: B — HCPCS Level II.** Injectable drugs/supplies are reported with **HCPCS Level II "J" codes** (e.g., J1100 for dexamethasone sodium phosphate). The *administration* is a CPT code; the *drug* is HCPCS. Mixing these up is a top cause of injection-claim denials.

Get denial-proof coding reps → zarivo.in

### QOTD #5 — Modifier 24 (global period)
**🧠 CPC Question of the Day**
During the 90-day global period after a knee replacement, the surgeon sees the patient for an *unrelated* ear infection and performs an E/M. Which modifier goes on that E/M?

A) 25
B) 57
C) 24
D) 79

**Answer: C — Modifier 24.** Modifier 24 = an *unrelated* E/M during a postoperative global period. (79 = unrelated *procedure* in the global period; 57 = decision for surgery; 25 = same-day E/M with a procedure.) Knowing your global-period modifiers cold is pure exam points.
